## 2. B2B SaaS PRICING FOR FINANCIAL SERVICES INTELLIGENCE

### Typical Price Ranges for Niche Intelligence Platforms

- **Entry-level CI software:** $10–$100/user/month ($120–$1,200/yr)
- **Mid-market CI platforms (Crayon, Klue, Contify):** $15K–$60K/yr
- **Premium financial intelligence (AlphaSense, CB Insights):** $40K–$200K+/yr
- **Analyst firms (Gartner, Forrester, Celent):** $25K–$500K+/yr
- **Consulting engagements (comparable alternative):** $50K–$500K+ per engagement

### What Pricing Models Work?

| Model | Pros for Living Intelligence | Cons |
|-------|------------------------------|------|
| **Per-firm license (flat annual fee)** | Simple; encourages internal adoption; no seat-counting friction | Harder to expand revenue per account |
| **Per-seat** | Easy to understand; natural expansion path | Discourages sharing; creates adoption friction; casual users feel overcharged |
| **Tiered by firm size (AUM bands)** | Aligns price with ability to pay; feels fair | Complex to administer; may alienate smaller firms |
| **Usage-based** | Pay-for-what-you-use appeal | Unpredictable revenue; budget uncertainty for buyers |
| **Hybrid (base + seats)** | 65% of SaaS leaders now use hybrid models; 25% higher NRR than pure per-seat | More complex to explain |

### Typical Enterprise Deal Sizes for CI Tools
- **Single team/department:** $20K–$50K/yr
- **Multi-department enterprise:** $50K–$200K/yr
- **Enterprise-wide with advisory:** $100K–$500K+/yr
- **The CI tools market** is $590M (2025), growing at 20% CAGR to $1.46B by 2030

### How McKinsey/BCG Price Research

- McKinsey and BCG publish most research **free** as thought leadership (lead generation for consulting)
- Their paid value is in **consulting engagements:** $50K–$500K+ per project
- Annual advisory retainers: $100K–$1M
- Living Intelligence occupies a gap: **more actionable than free MBB thought leadership, far cheaper than hiring them**

## 3. HOW WEALTH MANAGEMENT FIRMS BUY TECHNOLOGY/INTELLIGENCE

### The Buyer Profile

| Role | Budget Authority | Likelihood to Buy Living Intelligence |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Chief AI Officer / Head of AI** | High — emerging role, owns AI strategy budget | **PRIMARY BUYER** — directly needs to know what competitors are doing with AI |
| **Chief Digital Officer (CDO)** | High — owns digital transformation budget | **PRIMARY BUYER** — responsible for digital competitive positioning |
| **Head of Strategy** | Medium-High — owns strategic planning budget | **STRONG** — needs competitive landscape for strategic planning cycles |
| **CTO / CIO** | High — owns technology budget (5–10% of revenue) | **MODERATE** — more focused on build/buy decisions, but needs intel |
| **Head of Innovation** | Medium — smaller discretionary budget | **MODERATE** — research-oriented, often an influencer not decision-maker |
| **COO** | Medium — increasingly owns AI/data strategy | **MODERATE** — operational lens, needs intel for transformation programs |

### Budget Source

- **Most likely: Strategy & Planning budget** — competitive intelligence is a strategic function
- **Alternative: Technology budget** — 5–10% of revenue at wealth management firms; 20–40% allocated to "change the business"
- **Alternative: Research budget** — if the firm has a dedicated research/market intelligence function
- **Generative AI budget allocation:** 4–7% of tech budget specifically for AI tools/licensing
- **Key threshold:** A $10K–$50K annual subscription falls **below most enterprise procurement thresholds** for formal RFP — this is often a discretionary purchase by a senior leader

### Procurement Process

| Deal Size | Process | Timeline | Who Approves |
|-----------|---------|----------|--------------|
| **<$10K/yr** | Credit card or P.O. | Days to weeks | Individual budget holder |
| **$10K–$25K/yr** | Budget holder approval + vendor assessment form | 2–6 weeks | Department head |
| **$25K–$50K/yr** | Procurement involvement, vendor assessment, possibly competitive eval | 4–12 weeks | VP/SVP level + procurement |
| **$50K–$100K/yr** | Formal vendor assessment, security review, possibly RFP | 2–6 months | C-suite + procurement |
| **>$100K/yr** | Full RFP, multiple stakeholders, board awareness | 3–12 months | C-suite + board |

### Justification Requirements for $10K–$50K Spend

At this price point, the buyer typically needs:
1. **A one-page business case** — not a full ROI model
2. **Comparison to alternatives** — "This replaces X hours of analyst time" or "This is 10x cheaper than a Gartner subscription"
3. **Quick proof of value** — a sample report or demo that the buyer can share internally
4. **Alignment to a strategic priority** — "AI competitive positioning" is top-of-mind for every wealth management firm in 2026
5. **Low switching cost argument** — annual contract with easy exit reduces perceived risk

## 4. TRIAL AND CONVERSION MODELS FOR ENTERPRISE INTELLIGENCE

### What Works for B2B Intelligence Platforms

| Model | Best For | Conversion Rate | Used By |
|-------|----------|----------------|---------|
| **Gated sample report / sample intelligence** | High-value niche content | High intent leads | Gartner, Forrester, CB Insights |
| **Limited demo environment** | Complex platforms | Qualified pipeline | AlphaSense, PitchBook |
| **Free tier with upgrade** | Self-serve products | 2.6% organic, 18.5% from paid traffic | Not typical for enterprise intelligence |
| **Time-limited full trial** | Products with fast time-to-value | 15–30% (B2B SaaS average) | Klue, some CI tools |
| **"Founding member" early access** | New platforms building credibility | Very high — exclusivity drives conversion | Best fit for Living Intelligence |

### Recommended Model for Living Intelligence

**NOT a free trial. Instead: Gated Sample + Founding Member Program.**

Rationale:
- Intelligence content has **zero marginal cost to distribute but high perceived value** — giving it away free cheapens the brand
- Enterprise buyers don't want to "try" intelligence — they want to **see proof of quality**
- A gated sample (2–3 real intelligence entries + landscape snapshot) demonstrates value without giving away the store
- The "Founding Member" frame creates urgency and exclusivity

### Optimal Trial/Demo Structure

1. **Website:** Show enough to prove quality — 1 redacted intelligence entry, landscape overview, methodology explanation
2. **Gated access:** Email capture for a "Sample Intelligence Briefing" — 3 full entries + landscape for their specific firm
3. **Sales conversation:** 15-minute call to walk through the sample and discuss their specific needs
4. **Founding Member offer:** Lock in early pricing, annual commitment
5. **Onboarding:** Full access within 24 hours of signing

### Conversion Benchmarks
- B2B SaaS average trial-to-paid: **18.5%** (median), 35–45% (top quartile)
- For products with ACV >$50/month: trials convert **40–60% higher** than freemium
- 14-day trials work for simple products; **30-day** is standard for complex B2B
- For Living Intelligence: **skip the trial entirely** — go straight to gated sample + sales conversation

## 5. CFO JUSTIFICATION

### ROI Framework for Intelligence Platform Purchases

**The ROCI (Return on Competitive Intelligence) Framework:**
- Benefits = what the buyer says they are, measured against their Critical Success Indicators
- Track: research turnaround time, decision velocity, cost savings from automation

### Three Justification Angles

#### Angle 1: "Replace the Analyst"
> "One internal CI analyst costs $120K–$200K fully loaded. Living Intelligence delivers equivalent competitive monitoring for a fraction of that cost — and it's always on, always current."

- Internal analyst: $120K–$200K/yr (salary + benefits + tools + overhead)
- Living Intelligence: $6K–$18K/yr
- **Savings: $100K–$180K/yr, or 90%+**

#### Angle 2: "Replace the Consulting Engagement"
> "A single competitive strategy engagement from McKinsey or BCG costs $100K–$500K and delivers a point-in-time snapshot. Living Intelligence delivers continuous intelligence for a fraction of one engagement."

- One consulting engagement: $50K–$500K (one-time, stale in months)
- Living Intelligence: $6K–$18K/yr (continuous, always current)
- **10x–50x more cost-effective than consulting**

#### Angle 3: "Cost of Not Knowing"
> "Every major wealth management firm is investing in AI. Missing a competitor's move — a new partnership, a capability launch, a strategic hire — can mean falling 12–18 months behind. Living Intelligence ensures you never miss a signal."

- Cost of a missed competitive move: $1M–$10M+ in lost positioning
- Cost of Living Intelligence: $6K–$18K/yr
- **Insurance policy framing: trivial cost vs. existential risk**

- **Gartner:** "Reduce risk of wrong technology decisions" — risk mitigation
- **Klue:** "Win more competitive deals" — direct revenue attribution
- **Living Intelligence should use:** All three — time savings + risk mitigation + competitive advantage

### Sample Internal Business Case (for the buyer to forward)

## 6. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY FOR ENTERPRISE

### Should Pricing Be on the Website?

**The data says show it, but with nuance:**

- 62% of B2B buyers disqualify vendors who don't show pricing before engaging with sales
- Companies showing transparent pricing see **2–3x higher demo request rates**
- However: **every single comparable platform in this space hides pricing** (AlphaSense, CB Insights, Gartner, Klue, Crayon — all "Contact Sales")

**Recommendation for Living Intelligence: Show indicative pricing, hide exact numbers.**

Example:
> "Plans from $500/month. See our pricing tiers below. Enterprise pricing available."

This gives buyers budget confidence without inviting pure price comparison. It also signals that you're not afraid to show price — a mark of confidence that the product delivers value.

### Annual vs. Monthly

- **Standard for this category: annual contracts only**
- Every comparable platform (AlphaSense, CB Insights, Gartner, Klue, Crayon) sells annual
- Monthly billing **is not expected** by enterprise buyers and may actually reduce perceived seriousness
- **Recommendation: Annual only**, with optional quarterly for Founding Members if needed to reduce friction

### Seat-Based vs. Firm-Based Licensing

**Recommendation: Firm-based (company license), not per-seat.**

Rationale:
1. Living Intelligence is consumed by a small team (2–8 people typically: Head of AI, Head of Strategy, CDO, CTO, and their direct reports)
2. Per-seat pricing **discourages sharing** — you WANT the intelligence to circulate internally
3. Firm-based pricing is **simpler to sell** and avoids procurement friction over seat counts
4. You can tier by firm size (see below) rather than seat count
5. Companies using non-seat models show **25% higher net revenue retention**

### Founder/Early-Access Pricing Strategy

**"Founding Member" pricing is the proven playbook for new B2B intelligence platforms.**

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Naming** | "Founding Member" (not "early access" or "beta" — those sound unfinished) |
| **Price lock** | Founding rate locked for life (or 3 years minimum) — as long as subscription remains active |
| **Scarcity** | "Limited to the first 25 firms" — creates urgency, signals exclusivity |
| **Discount from future pricing** | 20–40% below planned standard pricing |
| **Commitment** | Annual contract, paid upfront or quarterly |
| **Extras** | Founding Members get: direct feedback channel, priority on new features, "Advisory Board" status |

### Three Tiers

| | **Founding Member** | **Standard** | **Enterprise** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $500/month ($6,000/yr) | $1,250/month ($15,000/yr) | Custom ($25,000–$50,000/yr) |
| **Status** | First 25 firms only, locked rate | Standard pricing | Multi-team, advisory included |
| **Intelligence entries** | Full access | Full access | Full access + custom alerts |
| **Landscape matrix** | Full access | Full access | Full access + custom dimensions |
| **Thought leadership** | Full access | Full access | Full access + exclusive briefings |
| **Users** | Up to 5 (firm license) | Up to 10 (firm license) | Unlimited (firm license) |
| **Updates** | Real-time | Real-time | Real-time + priority |
| **Extras** | Advisory Board status, direct feedback, rate locked for life | Standard support | Dedicated analyst, quarterly briefings, custom research |

### Why These Price Points

1. **$500/month Founding:** Below the threshold where most execs need procurement approval. Positions as "less than a Bloomberg terminal for one person." Comparable to Contify's entry point ($15K–$30K) but at the low end. Creates a no-brainer for the first 25 firms.

2. **$1,250/month Standard:** Aligns with the $15K–$20K/yr sweet spot for single-team CI purchases. Significantly cheaper than AlphaSense ($10K–$20K per SEAT), Klue ($16K–$43K), or CB Insights ($50K+). Defensible value: "Less than 10% of an analyst's salary for always-on competitive intelligence."

3. **$25K–$50K Enterprise:** Enters the range where Gartner, Forrester, and CB Insights compete. Justifies with multi-team access, custom research, and dedicated analyst time. At this level, you're competing with (and replacing) a single consulting engagement.

```

$100K+ ─── CB Insights / Forrester enterprise
 $50K  ─── LIVING INTELLIGENCE Enterprise ◄

 $15K  ─── LIVING INTELLIGENCE Standard ◄
 $15K  ─── Contify
  $6K  ─── LIVING INTELLIGENCE Founding ◄
```

Living Intelligence sits **deliberately below** the established players, signaling value without signaling "cheap." The Founding Member rate is an acquisition tool, not the long-term price.

## KEY STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Lead with the Founding Member program.** The first 25 firms at $500/month. This is your beachhead. Once you have 15–25 logos, you have the social proof to charge $1,250/month standard.

2. **Firm-based licensing, not per-seat.** Eliminate adoption friction. Let the intelligence circulate. Tier by firm size if needed.

3. **Annual contracts only.** Monthly billing signals consumer product, not enterprise intelligence. Offer quarterly payment if needed to reduce upfront friction.

4. **Show indicative pricing on the website.** "From $500/month" with tier descriptions. Hide exact enterprise pricing behind "Contact us." This is differentiated from competitors who all hide everything.

5. **Gated sample, not free trial.** Offer a sample intelligence briefing (3 entries + landscape snapshot personalized to their firm) as the conversion tool. This proves quality without cheapening the product.

6. **Build the CFO justification into your sales materials.** Provide a one-page "Business Case" template that buyers can forward to their procurement team. Include the "Replace the Analyst" and "Replace the Consultant" angles.

7. **Price anchoring matters.** Always compare to what they're currently spending: a Gartner sub ($68K median), a consulting engagement ($100K+), or an internal analyst ($150K+). Living Intelligence at $6K–$15K/yr is a fraction of any alternative.

8. **Plan for price increases.** Founding Members stay locked. Standard pricing should increase 10–15% annually as content depth grows. This is normal in this market (AlphaSense increased enterprise pricing 48% in one year).
